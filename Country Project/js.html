Food

The Vietnamese cuisine is aboutthe cooking of the green vegetable, fresh meat or fish using fresh herbs and spices to makedelicious dishes to eat with the steamed rice. A typical home meal often includes rice with a stir-fried vegetable with garlic, pork stew with ginger, and a tomato sour fish soup.


Main Dishes:


• Phở : A flavorful noodle soup with broth, rice noodles, herbs, and meat (typically beef or chicken). Served with fresh lime, chili, bean sprouts, and basil on the side.


• Bánh Mì : A Vietnamese baguette sandwich filled with a variety of meats (pork, chicken, or pate), pickled vegetables, cucumber, cilantro, and chili.


• Bánh Xèo : A crispy savory pancake made from rice flour, coconut milk, and turmeric, filled with shrimp, pork, bean sprouts, and green onions.Wrapped in lettuce and dipped in fish sauce.


Side Dishes:


• Chả Giò : Crispy rolls filled with minced pork, shrimp, mushrooms, and glass noodles, wrapped in rice paper and deep-fried.


• Bánh Cuốn : Steamed rice noodle rolls filled with ground pork and wood ear mushrooms, topped with fried shallots.


• Nem Nướng : Grilled pork sausage skewers, often served with rice paper and herbs for wrapping.


Desserts


• Bánh Da Lợn : A layered steamed cake made from rice flour, mung bean, and pandan leaves.


• Bánh Chuối : Banana cake made with rice flour and coconut milk, often steamed or baked.


• Chè : A sweet dessert soup or pudding made with beans, jellies, fruits, and coconut milk.
